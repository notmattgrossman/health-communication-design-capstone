End Notes

“Sometimes, magic is just someone spending more time on something than anyone else might reasonably expect.”

Teller

This project is the culmination of my 3 years of work in the Health Communication Design Studio and Research Lab. I owe so much gratitude to the professors, researchers, and designers who took a chance on me in my first-year and gave me the opportunity to be a part of the iHeard project. While I tried to capture my most important lessons learned in this project, it would be impossible to convey everything I’ve learned over the last three years. Thank you to Penina Laker and Christine Watridge in the HCDS and to Rachel Garg, Alexis Marsh, and Matt Kreuter in the HCRL. Your support and mentorship has been invaluable!

A special thank you to Heather Corcoran for her support durring the Integrated Design Project capstone class. And thank you to my BA design peers who provided so much of the feedback that guided this project.

This project was researched, written, designed, illustrated, and developed by Matt Grossman in 2025 for the Integrated Design Project capstone class at Washington University in St. Louis.
